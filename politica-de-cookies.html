Our Cookies Policy

The LSSI-CE (Spanish Information Society Services Act) requires all website owners to inform users of the existence of cookies, provide information about them, and request permission to download them.

As stated in Article 22.2 of Law 34/2002: "Service providers may use data storage and retrieval devices on terminal equipment of recipients, provided that they have given their consent after having been provided with clear and complete information about their use."

As the person responsible for this website, it is not always possible to have up-to-date information on the cookies that third parties may use through this site. This applies especially when this website contains integrated elements — texts, documents, images or short films stored elsewhere but displayed on our site. If you encounter cookies on this website not listed below, please let us know.

What are Cookies?

Cookies are a tool used by web servers to store and retrieve information about their visitors. They are simply a text file that some servers ask our browser to write on our hard drive, with information about what we have been doing on their pages. They have an expiry date, which can range from the duration of the session to a specified future date.

1. Cookies used by this website

This website uses both own and third-party cookies to provide a better browsing experience, allow content sharing on social networks, display ads based on your interests, and obtain user statistics.

The cookies used are associated only with an anonymous User and their computer. They do not provide references that allow the User's name and surname to be inferred, and cannot read data from their hard drive or include viruses.

You may freely decide whether to allow the cookies used on this website to be stored on your hard drive. You can configure your browser to accept or reject cookies by default, or to receive an on-screen warning upon receipt of each cookie.

Previo — ¿Qué son las Cookies?

Las cookies constituyen una herramienta empleada por los servidores Web para almacenar y recuperar información acerca de sus visitantes. No es más que un fichero de texto que algunos servidores piden a nuestro navegador que escriba en nuestro disco duro, con información acerca de lo que hemos estado haciendo por sus páginas. Poseen una fecha de caducidad, que puede oscilar desde el tiempo que dure la sesión hasta una fecha futura especificada.
